### 3.2 Quantum Measurement Theory

#### Von Neumann Measurement Scheme

**Three types of evolution:**
1. **Process 1:** Wave function collapse (discontinuous)
2. **Process 2:** Unitary evolution (continuous, Schrödinger equation)
3. **Process 3:** Observer's consciousness

**Bibliographic References:**
- von Neumann, J. (1932). *Mathematische Grundlagen der Quantenmechanik*. Springer.
- London, F., & Bauer, E. (1939). "La Théorie de l'Observation en Mécanique Quantique."

#### Interpretations of Quantum Mechanics

**Major interpretations:**
1. **Copenhagen:** Collapse upon measurement
2. **Many-Worlds:** No collapse, branching universes
3. **Pilot-Wave (de Broglie-Bohm):** Deterministic hidden variables
4. **QBism:** Quantum states are personal beliefs
5. **Relational:** Properties exist only relative to other systems

**Bibliographic References:**
- Everett, H. (1957). "'Relative State' Formulation of Quantum Mechanics." *Reviews of Modern Physics*, 29(3), 454-462.
- Bohm, D. (1952). "A Suggested Interpretation of the Quantum Theory." *Physical Review*, 85(2), 166-193.
- Fuchs, C. A., et al. (2014). "An Introduction to QBism." *arXiv:1311.5253*.
- Rovelli, C. (1996). "Relational Quantum Mechanics." *International Journal of Theoretical Physics*, 35(8), 1637-1678.

## 4. Awareness as Computational Element

### 4.1 Concept Description

The repository suggests awareness functions as a computational element within its systems.

### 4.2 Computational Theory of Mind

#### Classical Computationalism

**Key proponents:**
- Turing (computation)
- Putnam (functional states)
- Fodor (language of thought)

**Core thesis:** Mental states are computational states.

**Bibliographic References:**
- Turing, A. M. (1950). "Computing Machinery and Intelligence." *Mind*, 59(236), 433-460.
- Putnam, H. (1967). "The Nature of Mental States." *Art, Mind, and Religion*.
- Fodor, J. A. (1975). *The Language of Thought*. Harvard University Press.

#### Critiques

**Chinese Room Argument (Searle):**
Syntax (computation) is not sufficient for semantics (understanding).

**Bibliographic References:**
- Searle, J. R. (1980). "Minds, Brains, and Programs." *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*, 3(3), 417-424.

#### Modern Approaches

**Embodied cognition:**
Cognition emerges from body-environment interaction.

**Enactivism:**
Cognition is enacted through sensorimotor coupling.

**Bibliographic References:**
- Varela, F. J., et al. (1991). *The Embodied Mind*. MIT Press.
- Clark, A. (2008). *Supersizing the Mind*. Oxford University Press.

### 4.3 Consciousness in Computation
